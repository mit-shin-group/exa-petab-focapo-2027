questioning whether `Optim.IPNewton` (the original `petab_*` baseline) is a fair/rigorous
comparator. Ended by switching the canonical baseline to **Fides** and surfacing a fundamental
protocol issue (start point).

## Experiments & findings

### 1. Ipopt (exact Hessian) as same-algorithm-class baseline, tol=1e-6
- 11/20 converge; **9 fail with `Maximum_WallTime_Exceeded`** (status −5).
- But all 9 **found the optimum (or better)** — gap to best-known ≈1e-6%–1e-8%; Borghans/Zheng
  found *strictly better* optima than IPNewton. They only failed to **certify the KKT residual**.
- Mechanism (Crauste, `print_level=5`): objective hits optimum by iter 3, but `inf_du` floors ~2
  then oscillates to 1e4; barrier `mu` stuck (`lg(mu)=-1`); heavy regularization. Classic
  interior-point failure to certify a **bound-active / ill-conditioned reduced optimum**.

### 2. IPNewton "always converges" is a lax stop, not better solving
- Strict test (Crauste, f/x criteria disabled, g_tol-only): IPNewton reports `converged=true` at
  **gradient norm ≈ 35** (not 1e-6) — it stops on the FTOL objective-plateau. It physically cannot
  reach 1e-6 stationarity on the reduced problem (same wall as Ipopt). So IPNewton is NOT a rigorous
  baseline.
- **Metric fact:** MadNLP `tol` and Ipopt `tol` are both the **scaled-KKT error** (matched, fair).
  IPNewton `g_tol` is a gradient-only norm (different, and lax). Fides uses gradient/function
  trust-region criteria (different again).

### 3. exa failure taxonomy: T vs R (first-principles, from logs)
- **T (timeout) = size.** Bachmann (3.36M vars), Lucarelli (598k vars) — the two largest NLPs;
  wall-bound with `constr_viol` still large (4.5, 6.5) → never reached feasibility. MadNLP AND Ipopt
  both time out here.
- **R (restoration) = stationarity stagnation at a feasible point.** Borghans (22k), Elowitz (30k)
  are small (comparable to solved models). Elowitz reaches `inf_pr=3e-7` (feasible) but `inf_du`
  stuck ~100; enters feasibility restoration (pointless — already feasible) and fails at iter 320.
  Not size, not infeasibility — a certification dead-end. (Root cause of the indefinite KKT not
  measured to condition-number level.)
- Bound-active optima correlate with the trouble: every T/R model has 2–5 (Zheng 23!) estimated
  params on a bound at the optimum. But bound-activity is necessary-ish, not sufficient — many
  bound-active models still certify to full `0` (Armistead 5, Zheng-GPU 23). 0A/failure is driven by
  bounds **plus** ill-conditioning/backend.

### 4. Fix attempt: adaptive barrier (Ipopt `mu_strategy=adaptive` ↔ MadNLP `QualityFunctionUpdate`)
- adaptive μ + **1e-5**: still fails Crauste (inf_du floors ~1e-4). adaptive μ + **1e-4**: Crauste
  converges (status 0, 69s). Plain monotone+1e-4 still fails → the fix (adaptive) is necessary.
- Full gate (8 prior failures at adaptive+1e-4): only **2 more recovered** (Laske, Sneyd) → **14/20**.
  6 still fail: eval-cost-limited (Bachmann/Lucarelli/SalazarCavazos/Borghans) + oscillation
  (Zhao/Zheng). **No single matched knob gets Ipopt to 20/20.** → reconvened.

### 5. Decision: arbitrate on the solution, not the optimality certificate
- Internal optimality conditions can't be matched across formulations/solver classes. Compare on
  the **objective** instead. BUT: using the best-known objective as a *stopping target* is an unfair
  **oracle** for PEtab that exa never gets. So: **each method runs to its own natural convergence;
  compare time + the objective each independently reaches.** Study = "exa vs best-practice PEtab
  (state-of-the-art)", not "who finds the global optimum."

### 6. Fides as the best-practice PEtab baseline (trust-region, no barrier)
- Smoke: Crauste (GN) 1 iter FTOL ✓; **Bachmann (BFGS) 129s ✓ — where Ipopt AND exa both failed.**
- Full Fides 1e-6 bench (20 models): **19/20 converge** (all FTOL). **Borghans = MAXTIME (10,051
  iters) → Fides also fails Borghans.** Borghans is hard for ALL three (exa restoration, Ipopt fail,
  Fides maxtime).

### 7. The protocol problem (the big takeaway)
- The PEtab benchmark **nominalValue = the published optimum.** Starting every solve there makes the
  reduced-problem methods (Fides/Ipopt/IPNewton) certify in ~1 iter (FTOL). Fides iter counts:
  **1 iter (Bruno, Crauste); ≤5 iters (Armistead, Rahman, Fiedler, Schwen, Boehm)** — 7/20 do
  essentially no optimization. exa must still converge the full collocation (12+ iters) even when
  params are optimal → exa looks comparable-to-slower except on large/GPU cases.
- **The official PEtab benchmark does NOT start at nominal** — it uses **Latin-Hypercube multistart
  (~1000 starts)** across the bounds (priors sampled from priors); nominal/best-known is only the
  *reference* for convergence (cost within 0.1 of best). Confirmed in
  `PEtab/src/parameter_estimation/startguesses.jl` (`get_startguesses`, default `LatinHypercubeSample`).
- ⇒ Our single-from-nominal protocol diverges from PEtab methodology AND structurally understates
  exa. **Open decision: move to a random/perturbed (LHS) start, shared between exa and Fides.**

## exa tol=1e-4 experiment — TRIED AND SCRAPPED
Motivation: 1e-4 is MadNLP's *default* tol for `SparseCondensedKKTSystem` (=eps^(1/4); condensation
halves achievable accuracy vs eps^(1/2)=1e-8 for the full KKT). `acceptable_tol` is a fixed absolute
fallback (default 1e-6), NOT relative to tol — our "100× looser than tol" was a custom convention.
Ran exa GPU+CPU at tol=1e-4 / acceptable=1e-6 (separate result dirs to avoid the read-modify-write
race; `--gcthreads=1` to keep per-process cores ~1).

**Result: 1e-4 DEGRADES estimate quality — scrapped.** Comparing fair `petab_obj` (exa params scored
on PEtab's accurate ODE solve), 1e-4 vs 1e-6:
- **Perelson: 432 vs 223** (~2× worse) — converged `SOLVE_SUCCEEDED` to a *wrong* estimate.
- **Armistead: INFEASIBLE (−227) vs −300** at 1e-6.
- Blasi mildly worse; Boehm/Rahman identical.
- Solve times NOT reliably faster (Boehm 4.2 vs 1.2s, Rahman 0.7 vs 0.35s slower; others faster).

Why: looser KKT tol + equality relaxation lets MadNLP stop with the collocation constraints (dynamics)
only loosely satisfied and parameters under-converged → scored on the true objective they're worse,
sometimes catastrophically. For a parameter-ESTIMATION method that's the wrong trade. **Decision: keep
exa at 1e-6** (better, trustworthy estimates). Reverted options.jl (BENCH_TOL=1e-6, ACCEPT_TOL=1e-4)
and run_examodels.jl (removed RESULTDIR override). Restored canonical `results/` (exa 1e-6 + Fides 1e-6)
from `debugging/results_tol1e-6/`. Also implies NOT doing a Fides-1e-4 run (same degradation risk).
